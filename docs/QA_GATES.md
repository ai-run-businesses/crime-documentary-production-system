# QA gates

An export fails if any hard gate fails.

## Story gate

- The first 30 seconds contain a complete human turn or legible event.
- The hook creates one clear unanswered question without summarizing the whole case.
- Every transition changes time, place, evidence, claim, or story state.
- Narration explains why a transition occurs when picture alone cannot.
- No clue is introduced without subject, discovery, anomaly, and meaning.
- No factual caveat is narrated merely to display research caution.

## Visual-relevance gate

For every shot ask:

- What exact spoken beat does this serve?
- What would a viewer identify in one second?
- Does it show the person currently being discussed?
- Is it evidence, context, reconstruction, or atmosphere?
- Does it need explanation to make sense?

Wrong-person imagery, generic filler, and obscure metaphor fail.

## Source-text gate

Watch the export frame by frame around every source change. Fail on any visible:

- unwanted Chinese caption or label;
- unrelated broadcaster or channel overlay;
- app/player UI;
- source caption beneath the program caption;
- partially cropped text fragment;
- untracked AI watermark or generated pseudo-text.

## Still/motion gate

- No default micro-shake, fake handheld movement, or unexplained zoom.
- An information shot remains long enough to identify, read, and understand.
- Generated motion has an action or state change, not just parallax.
- Narration normally sits over relevant moving footage.

## Graphics gate

- No repeated PPT pages.
- No full-screen card for information that could be a lower third or overlay.
- One hierarchy, one alignment system, one logo placement system.
- No tiny footnotes that the viewer cannot read at normal playback size.
- Text is added in post, not generated inside image/video models.

## Dialogue and caption gate

- Consequential speech is not cut mid-sentence.
- Translation preserves meaning and original audio remains audible where appropriate.
- Captions cover every essential line, including emergency calls and cross-talk.
- Caption baseline stays clear of the subject and player-safe bottom area.
- No permanent black subtitle bar unless it is part of source evidence.

## Audio gate

- No silent tail or missing channel.
- Narration, dialogue, music, and effects remain intelligible on laptop speakers.
- Music has a beginning, progression, and ending; it does not loop mechanically.
- Abrupt picture transitions use an audio bridge or explanatory sentence when needed.
- Silence is intentional and time-coded in the paper edit.

## Authenticity gate

- Claims are distinguishable from verified facts.
- Generated or reconstructed material is continuously labeled where necessary.
- Real mouths are not altered to fabricate English speech.
- Real people are not renamed without an explicit editorial reason and prominent disclosure.
- No generated visual is allowed to imply a nonexistent piece of evidence.

## Export gate

- Correct duration, resolution, frame rate, aspect ratio, and audio sample rate.
- No black flash, freeze, missing frame, truncated line, or accidental one-second card.
- Contact sheet inspected at regular intervals and every scene boundary.
- Entire video watched once with sound and once muted; audio listened to once without picture.
