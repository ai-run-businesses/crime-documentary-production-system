# Visual grammar

## Asset priority

Use the highest available layer that serves the beat:

1. Primary moving evidence or recorded speech.
2. Primary still/document/photo.
3. Official or independently verifiable location/context footage.
4. Editorial map, timeline, crop, highlight, or diagram built from verified facts.
5. Clearly labeled generic reconstruction.
6. Atmosphere only when no more specific visual exists and the beat is transitional.

## Moving picture under narration

Default to relevant motion under narration. The motion can be quiet:

- a surveillance clip continuing after the key moment;
- an interview subject listening or pausing;
- investigators moving through a location;
- a slow exterior establishing shot;
- a document being handled or a line being highlighted;
- a route trace progressively closing.

Do not freeze a video merely because narration starts.

## Stills

A still can hold when it is the point: a victim portrait, last confirmed image,
letter, receipt, photograph, or court page. Do not apply automatic jitter or
micro-zoom. If motion is useful, it must reveal information: a crop widens to
show context, a second image enters, a line is highlighted, or a timestamp is isolated.

## Information-shot duration

- Sub-1.5 seconds: texture or non-readable montage only.
- 2–3 seconds: one recognizable object or face, no reading required.
- 3–5 seconds: one short label or simple relationship.
- 5–8 seconds: a document excerpt, two-person comparison, or evidence state change.
- Longer: only when spoken testimony, emotion, or complex evidence is still developing.

If the viewer must identify a person, read text, and understand a relationship,
one second is never sufficient.

## Source cleanup

Reject a frame containing unrelated:

- Chinese captions;
- broadcaster bugs or scraped-channel marks;
- `FZTV法治`, `视频素材来源于网络`, app UI, or embedded social captions;
- another creator's narrative overlays;
- player controls or browser chrome.

Preferred remedies, in order:

1. Find the clean/original source.
2. Use a different clean range.
3. Editorially reframe while preserving material context.
4. Rebuild a still from verified photos and facts.
5. Obscure a small peripheral mark only when doing so does not misrepresent provenance.

Record the original source in the ledger even when the final crop removes its text.

## Aspect ratio

For vertical or 4:3 evidence inside 16:9:

- keep the complete evidence image centered;
- fill the sides with a blurred, darkened enlargement of the same frame;
- preserve the original aspect and do not crop out evidentiary details;
- avoid plain black bars unless historical presentation calls for them.

## Text

- One idea per overlay.
- Use 3–7 words for emphasis whenever possible.
- Place text over moving footage when it clarifies the footage.
- Do not create a blue title page every time a date, question, or phrase appears.
- Dialogue subtitles use no permanent opaque backplate; use shadow or localized gradient.
- Full-screen cards are reserved for rare chapter punctuation, not ordinary exposition.

## Reconstruction

Allowed:

- anonymous corridor, exterior, object, empty room, or procedural action;
- generic imagery that communicates mood or missing connective tissue;
- enhanced archival imagery when no new material detail is invented.

Not allowed:

- generated victim, suspect, child, attack, body, discovery, confession, or case-specific evidence presented as real;
- fake readable documents, timestamps, badges, agency insignia, or headlines;
- super-resolution that hallucinates identifying details and is then treated as evidence.

Label `VISUAL RECONSTRUCTION` or `ENHANCED ARCHIVAL FOOTAGE` unobtrusively for
the full duration when the distinction could matter.
