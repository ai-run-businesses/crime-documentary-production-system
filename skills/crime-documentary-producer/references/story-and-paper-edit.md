# Story and paper edit

## Case feasibility

Score the public asset package, not the written case summary.

- Recorded human conflict or contradiction.
- Multiple independent evidence modes.
- Three or more meaningful state changes.
- Central question with a visible or audible payoff.
- Enough usable footage to avoid long generic filler.

A shocking crime can still be a weak documentary if the public assets contain
only commentary, repeated stills, or a simple linear explanation.

## Raw story versus edited storyline

The raw story answers what happened in chronological order. The edited storyline
controls when the viewer learns each verified fact.

Do not manufacture uncertainty after the record resolves it. Suspense comes from
temporarily following the question investigators or witnesses actually faced.

## Paper-edit fields

For every beat include:

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Foreground audio | Raw speech, emergency call, narration, silence, or music-led beat |
| Story function | Hook, claim, contradiction, test, narrowing, reveal, consequence, reset |
| Source | Exact file and timecode or record page |
| Picture | What is visible and why it serves this audio |
| State change | What is different by the end |
| Cleanup | Source text, crop, blur, privacy, translation, label |
| Minimum hold | Time needed to identify, read, and understand |
| Audio bridge | J-cut, L-cut, room tone, music transition, or deliberate silence |

## Hook test

By 30 seconds, the viewer should have:

- one concrete person/action/object;
- one contradiction or unusual consequence;
- one proof cue;
- one unresolved question.

Do not deliver the full case summary during the hook.

## Transition test

A transition must change at least one of:

- time;
- place;
- speaker;
- evidence mode;
- claim status;
- search scope;
- legal status;
- emotional state.

If the reason for the change is not visible, bridge it with narration or audio.
