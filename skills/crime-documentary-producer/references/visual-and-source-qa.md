# Visual and source QA

## Moving footage under narration

Keep relevant source footage moving while narration interprets it. Freeze only
when the frozen frame is itself evidence. A still should hold cleanly unless a
crop, highlight, comparison, or reveal adds information.

## Information holds

- Under 1.5 s: non-readable texture only.
- 2–3 s: one face/object, no reading.
- 3–5 s: one short label or simple relationship.
- 5–8 s: document excerpt, comparison, or evidence state change.
- Longer: testimony, emotion, or complex evidence still developing.

## Source-text sweep

Fail the master on any unintended:

- Chinese caption, label, or sign introduced by the source edit;
- broadcaster/channel bug or scraped-video ownership mark;
- source subtitle beneath program captions;
- app/player/browser UI;
- cropped text fragment;
- generated pseudo-text or untracked AI watermark.

Find a clean source first. Otherwise choose a clean range, reframe, rebuild a
still from verified material, or obscure only a small peripheral mark without
misrepresenting provenance.

Sample both ends of every excerpt. A clean in-point does not prove a clean
out-point: edited source compilations may switch to an unrelated visual or add a
new subtitle or watermark before the selected shot ends.

For footage with embedded subtitles, inspect several frames after speech begins.
If source text cannot be removed cleanly, crop or reframe the moving shot, or use
another truthful visual. Never stack source subtitles under narrator captions.

## Visual relevance

For every shot ask:

1. What exact audio beat is it serving?
2. What action or situation is legible in one second?
3. Is the person currently discussed actually the visual subject?
4. Is this evidence, context, reconstruction, or atmosphere?
5. Does it require editor explanation to make sense?
6. Does a transition briefly retain the previous subject after a new sentence has
   already named someone else?

Regenerate or replace wrong-person imagery, generic filler, and obscure metaphor.

## Aspect-ratio treatment

Keep vertical or 4:3 evidence centered at its native aspect. Fill unused 16:9
space with a blurred, darkened enlargement of the same frame. Do not crop out
material evidence merely to fill the canvas.

## Reconstruction

Use only for generic connective tissue or processes that cannot be shown.
Label it continuously. Never reconstruct a real victim, suspect, child, attack,
body, confession, discovery, or piece of evidence as if it were captured footage.

## Captions

- Complete coverage for essential dialogue and emergency calls.
- Maximum two lines when possible.
- No permanent black bottom bar.
- Use shadow or a localized feathered gradient.
- Keep the subject and evidentiary details clear.
- Collision-test captions with lower-thirds and source-native timestamps in the
  final composite rather than approving each layer separately.

## Translated and lipsynced archival speech

Treat translated speech as an evidentiary transformation, not a cosmetic audio
replacement. Use it only when the production owner has explicitly approved the
experiment and the original-language source remains traceable.

Before rendering:

1. Lock a human-reviewed translation from the source-language transcript.
2. Verify every pronoun, name, number, negation, relationship, and modal verb.
3. Preserve the speaker's meaning without adding motive, certainty, or emotion.
4. Allocate enough picture time for the entire translated thought. Never speed,
   truncate, or cut away merely to fit a predetermined slot.

After rendering:

- independently transcribe the final English audio and compare it with the
  locked translation;
- reject any subject, pronoun, negation, name, number, or factual drift;
- sample lips, teeth, tongue, cheeks, and jaw densely throughout the altered
  excerpt rather than checking only the in- and out-frames;
- rerun the source-text sweep because lipsync or reframing may reveal previously
  hidden captions, bugs, or edge fragments;
- keep an explicit `AI-ASSISTED ENGLISH DUB` label visible for exactly the altered
  interval, and remove it immediately when authentic audio or narration resumes;
- keep the unaltered source, locked translation, generated voice file, final
  lipsync, and verification transcript together in the evidence package.

Prefer a licensed stock voice. Do not clone or imitate the real speaker's voice.
The label communicates the transformation; narration should not stop the story
to repeat a long disclaimer.
