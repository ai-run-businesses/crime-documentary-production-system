# Music and sound

## Compose behavior, not mood adjectives

Reject a prompt that asks only for `dark cinematic suspense`. Define audible
behavior:

- BPM or pulse range;
- whether pressure begins on frame one or develops later;
- rhythmic engine and instrumentation;
- a short repeatable warning or evidence motif;
- planned density changes;
- the exact ending behavior and duration;
- a negative list excluding uplifting harmony, heroic scoring, literal sirens,
  vocals, dance-pop, piano-led sentiment and arbitrary fades.

Intensity means forward rhythmic pressure, not maximum loudness. Preserve the
1–4 kHz narration band and do not imply action, danger or certainty beyond the
picture.

## Cold-open and brand workflow

1. Lock picture, narration and recorded dialogue first.
2. Measure the exact ending frame.
3. Generate at least three candidates. Vary the rhythmic concept and intensity,
   not merely the instrument names.
4. Normalize every complete picture-sync audition to the same integrated
   loudness before comparison. A practical review target is approximately
   -15 to -17 LUFS, with true peak below -1 dBFS.
5. Compare each cue against the same picture and voice mix.
6. Reject cues that need ten seconds to become interesting, could score a
   corporate explainer, mask consonants, end as a loop, or overstate the visible
   conflict.
7. Choose the least aggressive cue that still creates pressure.
8. Preserve the authored ending. Time-stretch a complete cue by no more than
   about 12 percent, or perform a bar-aligned sectional edit.

For a structured music model, use three functional sections:

1. immediate premise or impact;
2. investigative escalation with at least one density change;
3. precise unresolved brand sting with a controlled tail.

The section durations must sum to the locked picture duration.

## Episode scoring

Use a cue sheet by story function:

- intelligible confrontation or consequential raw speech: room tone or no score;
- contradiction/evidence discovery: restrained motif and low pulse;
- investigative bridge under narration: quiet rhythmic bed over relevant moving
  footage;
- actual escalation: add density and percussion only when the story escalates;
- aftermath: remove layers rather than resolving triumphantly.

Do not run one intense cue continuously. Music must have a beginning,
development and ending that correspond to evidence-state changes.

## Listening gate

Approval requires human listening to at least three equal-loudness picture-sync
auditions. Prompt quality, waveform density and automated mood labels do not
substitute for listening.
