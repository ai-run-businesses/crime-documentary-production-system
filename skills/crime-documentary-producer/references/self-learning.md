# Self-learning protocol

## Log first

For every human correction or analytics result, record:

- date and artifact;
- observation;
- evidence;
- affected stage;
- confidence;
- proposed change;
- whether it is case-specific or reusable.

## Rule states

- `Observed`: one piece of evidence.
- `Candidate`: repeated observation or strong golden-set support.
- `Promoted`: repeatable, actionable, and not contradicted by stronger evidence.
- `Retired`: later evidence shows the rule is harmful or too broad.

## Automatic trigger

After any of the following, review the learning log before closing the task:

- user gives time-coded feedback;
- a proof or final master fails QA;
- a new professional reference is analyzed;
- title/thumbnail test completes;
- first-30-second retention or average view duration becomes available;
- copyright, monetization, or disclosure handling changes.

Update the workflow when the learning is broadly reusable. Do not wait for the
user to explicitly request documentation maintenance.

## Guardrail

Engagement results may change pacing, packaging, or visual strategy. They must
not weaken sourcing, privacy, reconstruction labeling, or fact/claim separation.
